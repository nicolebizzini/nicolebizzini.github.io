# Research Experience 

I have worked in hospital-based and academic research environments focusing on physiological data analysis, biomedical systems reliability, and translational neuroengineering applications.

At NYU Langone Health (Neural Interface Engineering Lab), I contributed to in vivo data analysis supporting closed-loop ultrasound neuromodulation experiments, developing reproducible processing workflows and ensuring high standards of experimental reliability and documentation.

Alongside this, I have designed and developed a wearable respiratory monitoring device, integrating sensors and embedded systems for stable real-time signal acquisition, with attention to data integrity and potential clinical application.

I have also collected and curated multimodal physiological datasets (EMG, NIRS) across human experimental sessions, ensuring protocol compliance, participant safety, and audit-ready documentation.
